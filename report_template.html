How we approach
CV optimisation,

What we consider, and why it matters. Every CV we optimise is evaluated against two distinct stages of the hiring process. Both matter. Failing either one ends your application before it has begun.

Stage one
01

The technology

Before a recruiter reads a single word, your CV is processed by an Applicant Tracking System. ATS software parses your document and scores it against the job specification. If the right signals are not present, the CV is rejected automatically. No human sees it.

Those signals include formatting, keywords, job title alignment, section headings, achievement framing, and industry-specific terminology. Each of these has been addressed in your optimised CV.

Your CV has been saved as a Word document (.docx), the standard format for ATS submissions. PDF and PowerPoint files are not reliably parsed and can trigger an automatic rejection.

Keep it as .docx when applying.
Stage two
02

The human

If your CV clears the ATS stage, it reaches a recruiter or hiring manager. At this point, the challenge changes. Initial CV decisions are made within the first few seconds of opening a document.

In that window, the reader is scanning for one thing: evidence that this person can do what we need. Your optimised CV has been built around six things that earn attention beyond that first scan — executive summary, achievement framing, career progression clarity, relevance to the target role, consistency of tone, and the removal of anything working against you.

Each of these is covered in Section 02.

Why both
stages matter.

A CV that passes ATS but reads poorly to a human does not get an interview. A CV that reads well but fails ATS never reaches a human at all. Every decision made in your optimised CV - formatting, structure, language, and framing - has been made with both stages in mind.

The goal is straightforward: to get you in front of the right person, face to face, where you can do what no CV can do. Bring your story to life.
